\# üìä HR Data Analysis \& Visualization Project



\## Project Overview

This project analyzes a dataset containing over \*\*22,000 rows\*\* of Human Resources data ranging from the year \*\*2000 to 2020\*\*. The primary goal is to uncover insights regarding employee demographics, turnover trends, gender distribution, and tenure using SQL for data processing and PowerBI for visualization.



\## üõ† Tools Used

\* \*\*Data Cleaning \& Analysis:\*\* MySQL

\* \*\*Data Visualization:\*\* PowerBI

\* \*\*Source Data:\*\* HR Dataset (2000 - 2020)



\## üßπ Data Cleaning \& Limitations

Before analysis, a thorough data cleaning process was conducted to ensure accuracy:

\* \*\*Age Validation:\*\* 967 records with negative ages were excluded. Only employees aged 18 and above were included.

\* \*\*Term Date Validation:\*\* 1,599 records with termination dates far into the future were excluded. Only termination dates less than or equal to the current date were used.



\## üîç Key Questions Answered

The analysis focused on answering the following critical business questions:

1\.  What is the gender breakdown of employees in the company?

2\.  What is the race/ethnicity breakdown of employees?

3\.  What is the age distribution of employees?

4\.  How many employees work at headquarters versus remote locations?

5\.  What is the average length of employment for employees who have been terminated?

6\.  How does the gender distribution vary across departments and job titles?

7\.  What is the distribution of job titles across the company?

8\.  Which department has the highest turnover rate?

9\.  What is the distribution of employees across locations by state?

10\. How has the company's employee count changed over time based on hire and term dates?

11\. What is the tenure distribution for each department?



\## üìà Summary of Findings



\### Demographics

\* \*\*Gender:\*\* There are more male employees than female employees in the workforce.

\* \*\*Race/Ethnicity:\*\* The "White" racial group is the most dominant, while "Native Hawaiian" and "American Indian" are the least represented.

\* \*\*Age:\*\*

&nbsp;   \* Age range: 23 to 60 years old.

&nbsp;   \* The largest age group is \*\*35-44\*\*, followed by \*\*25-34\*\*. The smallest group is \*\*18-24\*\*.



\### Location

\* A significantly larger number of employees work at the \*\*Headquarters\*\* compared to remote locations.

\* A large concentration of employees resides in the state of \*\*Ohio\*\*.



\### Tenure \& Turnover

\* \*\*Average Tenure:\*\* The average length of employment for terminated employees is approximately \*\*8 years\*\*.

\* \*\*Turnover by Department:\*\*

&nbsp;   \* \*\*Highest Turnover:\*\* Marketing (followed by Training).

&nbsp;   \* \*\*Lowest Turnover:\*\* Research and Development, Support, and Legal.

\* \*\*Department Tenure:\*\* The Legal and Auditing departments have the highest average tenure, while Services, Sales, and Marketing have the lowest.



\### Growth

\* The net change in employee count has shown an increase over the years analyzed.
